### Trend Direction
- Identify the overall direction: Determine whether the chart is in an uptrend, downtrend, or sideways movement.
- Look for higher highs and higher lows for an uptrend, and lower highs and lower lows for a downtrend.

### Order Blocks
- Order blocks are areas where price consolidated before making a strong move. These can act as support or resistance.
- Look for consolidation followed by a sharp move up or down to identify potential order blocks.

### Fair Value Gaps (FVG)
- FVGs are gaps between consecutive price bars where no trades occurred. These gaps can be potential areas for price to revisit.
- Identify any visible gaps in the price bars and mark their levels.

### Swing Highs/Lows
- Swing highs are peaks in price followed by a decline, while swing lows are troughs followed by a rise.
- Use these to determine retracement levels and potential reversal points.

### Break of Structure (BOS) / Change of Character (CHoCH)
- BOS occurs when price breaks a previous high or low, indicating a potential trend continuation.
- CHoCH signals a potential trend reversal. It occurs when there's a break of structure in the opposite direction.

### Liquidity Sweeps
- A sweep of liquidity occurs when price moves beyond a recent high or low to capture stop losses before reversing.
- Look for sharp moves that are quickly rejected.
